Legend

The Vision of Enzo Ferrari

Enzo Ferrari, born in 1898, was a visionary who founded Ferrari in 1947. His passion for cars and racing led him to create one of the most iconic automotive brands in the world. As a leader, he was known for his relentless pursuit of excellence and innovation in the automotive industry.

"The car is one of the best machines that man has ever created."
1947

Foundation of Ferrari

Enzo Ferrari founded the Ferrari brand in Maranello, Italy, and introduced its first production car: the 125 S.

1951

First Formula 1 Victory

Ferrari won its first Formula 1 Grand Prix with driver José Froilán González.

1962

Launch of the Ferrari 250 GTO

The Ferrari 250 GTO became one of the most iconic and valuable cars in history.

1975

Ferrari 308 GTB

Introduction of the Ferrari 308 GTB, a model that would become an instant classic, popularized by the "Magnum P.I." TV series.

1990

Ferrari F40

The Ferrari F40, one of the most admired sports cars in history, was launched to celebrate Ferrari's 40th anniversary.

2002

Ferrari Enzo

The Ferrari Enzo was introduced as a limited-production model, representing the best in technology and performance.

2007

Formula 1 World Championship Victory

Ferrari won the Formula 1 Constructors' Championship with driver Kimi Räikkönen.

2015

Ferrari LaFerrari

Introduction of the Ferrari LaFerrari, a hybrid supercar that marked a new era in design and technology for cars.

2020

Ferrari SF90 Stradale

Introduction of the Ferrari SF90 Stradale, a high-performance hybrid with innovative technology and impressive design.

2021

Ferrari 296 GTB

Introduction of the Ferrari 296 GTB, a high-performance hybrid with a V6 turbo engine and next-generation hybrid technology.

2022

Ferrari Purosangue

Launch of the Ferrari Purosangue, the brand's first SUV, combining Ferrari's exceptional performance with the versatility of an SUV.

  • Ferrari's Legendary Models

    Performance  —  Innovation  —  Heritage

    Over 75 Years — Defining Automotive Excellence

    Racing Legacy > Supercar Evolution > Future Innovations

    Ferrari embodies speed, power, and engineering excellence. From Formula 1 dominance to exclusive supercars, it continues to redefine the automotive world.

  • Ferrari 250 GTO

    The Ultimate Racing Legend

    Introduced in 1962, the Ferrari 250 GTO became one of the most iconic and sought-after cars in automotive history.

    Powered by a 3.0L V12 engine, it dominated endurance races and solidified Ferrari’s reputation in motorsport.

    Only 36 units were ever built, making it one of the rarest and most valuable Ferraris, with auction prices reaching record-breaking figures.

  • Ferrari F40

    A True Supercar Icon

    Launched in 1987 to celebrate Ferrari’s 40th anniversary, the F40 became a symbol of raw performance.

    Equipped with a twin-turbocharged V8 engine, it delivered an impressive 478 hp and a top speed of 324 km/h.

    Designed for aerodynamic efficiency, it was the last Ferrari personally approved by Enzo Ferrari, making it a true legend.

  • Ferrari Enzo

    A Tribute to the Founder

    Introduced in 2002, the Ferrari Enzo was named in honor of Enzo Ferrari, the legendary founder of the brand.

    Featuring a 6.0L V12 engine, it produced 660 hp and could reach a top speed of 355 km/h.

    Inspired by Formula 1 technology, the Enzo featured advanced aerodynamics, an F1-style gearbox, and carbon fiber construction.

Legendary Ferrari Drivers

A tribute to the men who forged Ferrari's legacy

Juan Manuel Fangio

Considered one of the best drivers of all time, he won the world championship several times, leaving an indelible mark on Ferrari.

Dino Ferrari

The son of Enzo Ferrari, whose contribution to Ferrari's car designs was crucial. His legacy lives on in the brand.

Stirling Moss

One of the most distinguished drivers in history, known for his participation in Ferrari competitions, where he showed his skill.

Gilles Villeneuve

A true Ferrari hero, known for his bravery and aggressive driving style. His legacy remains alive in the heart of the tifosi.
